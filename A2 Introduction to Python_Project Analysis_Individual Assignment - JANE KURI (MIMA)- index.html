1. Introduction

This analysis explores patterns in bike usage from the perspectives of various rider groups. Key factors such as time of day, day of the week, weather, seasons, holidays, and rental behaviors are examined to understand what drives bike rental decisions. The goal is to identify the most favorable conditions that influence higher ridership and provide insight into user motivations. A survey by Fitch, Carlen, and Handy (2022) highlights that recognizing the environments in which riders feel most comfortable is critical to improving engagement. These insights are framed to support decision-making in a results-driven business context, especially for non-technical stakeholders.

2. Insights

Insight 1: Afternoon and Evening Hours Drive Peak Bike Rentals

The data reveals that bike rentals significantly increase during the late afternoon and early evening, particularly between 4 PM and 8 PM. This pattern suggests that many users are utilizing bikes for commuting purposes, aligning with typical workday end times. Such usage indicates a preference for biking as a convenient and efficient mode of transportation during rush hours. Also bike commuting, such as cycling to work, is positively associated with better cardiovascular health, lower obesity rates, and improved physical fitness among employed adults (Leung, A. Scheer, J. & Wennberg, H. (2025))

Thus, to capitalize on this trend, the company should ensure increased bike availability during these peak hours, possibly by reallocating resources or implementing dynamic pricing strategies. Additionally, promoting subscription models or loyalty programs targeted at daily commuters could enhance user retention and satisfaction.

Insight 2: Favorable Weather Conditions Boost Bike Rentals

Analysis indicates that bike rentals surge during mild weather conditions, particularly in spring, summer, and autumn. Optimal temperatures, low humidity, and clear skies encourage more individuals to choose biking as their mode of transport. Conversely, adverse weather conditions like rain, snow, or extreme temperatures deter users, leading to decreased rentals (Fitch, Carlen, and Handy (2022). Thus, the company should focus marketing efforts during favorable weather periods, perhaps by offering promotions or organizing events to attract users. During less favorable conditions, providing incentives or highlighting safety measures can help maintain user engagement. Additionally, integrating weather forecasts into operational planning can aid in resource allocation and demand prediction.

Insight 3: Holidays and Weekends See Reduced Bike Usage

The data shows a decline in bike rentals during holidays and weekends, suggesting that users primarily utilize bikes for weekday commuting. This trend implies that leisure or recreational biking is less prevalent among the user base. Understanding this behavior is crucial for optimizing operations and marketing strategies. Thus, to address the dip in usage during non-working days, the company could introduce leisure-focused campaigns, such as guided tours or family-friendly events. Collaborations with local attractions or parks can also encourage recreational biking. By diversifying offerings to cater to both commuters and leisure riders, the company can enhance overall utilization rates.

## Insight One: Visualizing How Weather Factors Correlate with Bike Rental Volumes

# Temperature and humidity affect rental volumes.

# specifying plot size (making it bigger)
fig, ax = plt.subplots( figsize = (10 , 8) )


# developing a spicy heatmap
sns.heatmap(df[['Temperature', 'Humidity', 'Wind Speed', 'Rainfall', 'Snowfall', 'RENTALS']].corr(), 
            cmap       = 'coolwarm', 
            square     = True , 
            annot      = True ,
            linecolor  = 'black', 
            linewidths = 0.5 ) 


# title and displaying the plot
plt.title(label = """
'Correlation between Weather Factors and Rentals'
""")

# showing the finished product
plt.show(block = True)

5.1. Analysis:

The correlation heatmap shows that temperature has the strongest positive impact on bike rentals (r = 0.55), meaning warmer days lead to higher rental activity. Humidity, snowfall, and rainfall negatively affect rentals, indicating lower usage during wet or cold conditions. Wind speed has a weaker but still negative correlation, suggesting that windy days may also discourage riders. Visibility and UV index show mild associations but are less influential than temperature and precipitation. Overall, weather plays a significant role in rental behavior, with favorable conditions like warmth and dryness driving higher usage.

## Insight 2: Bike rentals peak during specific hours, showing clear daily time preferences for riding

# Calculating average rentals by hour
hourly_rentals = df.groupby('Hour')['RENTALS'].mean().reset_index()

# Create regression scatterplot with seaborn
plot  = sns.lmplot(
    x = 'Hour',              # x-axis: Hour of the day
    y = 'RENTALS',           # y-axis: Average Rentals
    data = hourly_rentals,   # Use the hourly average data
    scatter = True,         # Show scatter points
    fit_reg = True,         # Add regression line
    aspect = 2              # Aspect ratio
)

# Apply labels using plot.ax, not plt directly
plot.ax.set_title ('Average Bike Rentals by Hour of the Day')
plot.set_axis_labels ('Hour', 'Average Rentals')
plot.set(xlim = (0, 23))  # Set correct x-axis limits for hours 023

# Layout adjustment and display
plt.tight_layout (pad = 2.0)
plt.show()

5.2. Analysis:

This chart shows that bike rentals steadily increase throughout the day, peaking in the late afternoon and early evening. Rental numbers are lowest between 3 AM and 6 AM, when most people are likely at home. The trend indicates that bicycles are primarily used for commuting or leisure after work or school hours. There is a noticeable rise in demand starting mid-morning and continuing through the evening. This pattern reflects a consistent user preference for afternoon and evening rides over early-morning usage.

## Insight Three: Seasonal Trends and Holiday Impact Significantly Influence Average Bike Rentals

# Average rentals by season and holiday

plt.xlabel('Holiday')
plt.ylabel('Average Rentals')
plt.show()

5.3. Analysis:

Bike rentals are highest in summer and autumn, indicating these are peak demand seasons due to favorable weather. Winter sees the lowest rentals, likely because colder temperatures reduce outdoor activity. The data also shows that rentals drop on holidays compared to regular days. This suggests that people may prefer staying indoors or using other transportation on holidays. Overall, seasonality and holidays significantly influence rental behavior.

6. Strategic recommendations:

Based on the visuals, bike rentals peak during summer and autumn, suggesting businesses should prioritize advertising, promotions, and bike availability in these seasons to meet demand. In contrast, rentals drop significantly in winter, making it an ideal time to scale down the fleet or focus on maintenance and off-season planning. Rentals are notably higher on non-holidays, so offering special promotions or discounts on holidays could help boost usage. Since the afternoon and early evening are peak hours, bikes should be fully restocked by midday to avoid lost opportunities. Aligning marketing, operations, and inventory with these patterns can enhance customer satisfaction and improve profitability.

7. Conclusion

Overall, the findings reveal clear patterns in how people use bikes depending on the time of year, weather, and day type. Warm and dry conditions tend to attract more riders, while colder and wet periods see a decline in activity. People are more likely to ride during certain times of day, especially in the late afternoon when theyre likely free from work or school. The drop in usage during holidays hints that different activities may take priority, or that fewer people are out and about. These patterns can guide better planning, promotions, and services that match when and how people actually want to ride.
